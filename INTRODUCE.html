Panic: Will Artificial Intelligence eventually replace humans?

--Misconceptions about AI should be corrected!

Background

From hypothesis to the most important technology in the world today, the potential of AI is being exploited for use at a rate several times faster, to the point where AI is ubiquitous in life, with coverage in a variety of different fields, including healthcare, marketing, robotics, the stock market, business analytics, transport, surveillance, and more (Lidströmer et al 2022).

Reason for concern

Because AI can accurately mimic almost any aspect of human learning or other features of intelligence, it is customary to focus attention on assessing whether AI is potentially dangerous as it transitions to intelligent beings (Burgard 2022 pp. 11-18), even to the point of fearing that evolving AI will generate self-minds (Durt 2022, pp. 67-82) and fearing that AI will crowd out human producer jobs and create unemployment dilemmas (Tan & Lim 2018).

What to worry about

Admittedly, the blurring of the boundaries between AI and humans was gradually realized as early as 1950 in the Turing Test proposed by Alan Turing (Durt 2022), and the billionaire industrialist Elon Musk has claimed that AI is a fundamental existential risk to humanity (Tan & Lim 2018).

Correction of a false claim

Nevertheless, the reality is that this claim is not rigorous, with Durt (2022) pointing out that the concept of AI pushed by people who believe that AI can give birth to self-awareness stems more from science fiction than concrete research.

A New Definition of AI

"It is misleading to define AI as something that mimics or replicates human intelligence," Huxor (2022) also explains further around the Turing Test, "The Turing Test treats all AI systems as a 'medium'- -a medium that allows humans to externalize their intelligence and communicate with others, rather than autonomous intelligent agents," and she argues that people are over-interpreting the purpose of the Turing Test because they are unfamiliar with AI.

The difference between AI and humans

Although AI is capable of processing large amounts of data and knowledge, it lacks human experience, feelings, and understanding of situations (Hanemaayer 2022). Based on this foundation, it can be concluded that Ai is still fundamentally different from human beings.
